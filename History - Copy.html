The History of Kanvashram


The Aashram
Kanvashram
Kanvashram
Once upon a time nearly 5000 years ago in Kanvashramthere was a Aashram of Maharishi Kanva and there was a Gurukul of Aashram where over 10,000 students used to get education by Maharishi Kanva. There in the Gurukul of Aashram students used to clean their Aashram at early morning. In the Aashram students ate food together and the food for students was come from over 175 villages of Kanvashram. Students lived in the Aashram.

According to Rigveda, the chancellor of this university was the great saint, and pracher Kanva - Mahrashi Kanvas started a new system of studying Vedas which is known as Kanva Shakha (branch of studying vedas). In this university, various philosphies, classification of Vedas, etc are taught to the pupils.Students are taught various subby various Gurus(Teacher) of the Aashram.

Maharishi Vishwamitra and Menka(The beauty woman of King Indra) had a girl Shakuntla and then Menka leaved Shakuntla at the Aashram of Maharishi Kanva. Maharishi Kanva give shelter to Shakuntla and Shakuntla was grown up in the Aashram of Maharishi Kanva.

Maharishi Kanva
The temple of Bharat
Shakuntla and King Dushyant
 One day the King of Hastinapur Dushyant came here in the Kanvashram. He was totally lost his heart by seeing the beauty of Kanvashram. When he was roaming in the jungles(Forest) of Kanvashram, he reached to the Aashram of Maharishi Kanva and in the Aashram he saw a girl Shakuntla and then they both Shakuntla and King Dushyant had married here.





They produced a child here and Maharishi Kanva give the name "Bharat" to the child of Shakuntla. Bharat grown up between the jungles(Forest) of Kanvashram. He used to play with the wild animals of the jungles like Lion, Deer, Tiger, Elephant, Birds, etc. The fact of playing of child Bharat with these wild animals is that he used to count the teeth of the king of forest "Lion".





And the most important thing is that from the name of the child "Bharat" our country India gets its name "Bharat" and also called up as Bharatvarsh.
