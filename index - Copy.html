"From where the Hindi name of India 'Bharat' derived from? Have you ever think about it?

I know that you don't know the answer of it. Now I'll tell you the answer of this question-

The Hindi name of our country India 'Bharat' derived from the place "Kanvashram". The name 'Bharat' has been placed as a name of India after the name of King Bharat whose birth place was "Kanvashram".

Kanvashram is situated at the foothills of Shivalik Ranges of Himalays. It have a wide range of area. Two or three cities like Kotdwara, Duggada, etc. and many semi-rural areas comes under the area of Kanvashram. The main Kanvashram is situated at the area of Bhabhar means just at the foot hills of Shivalik ranges. The word Kanvashram is made up of 2 words (Kanvashram = Kanva+Aasharm) means the Aashram of Mahrishi Kanva (Supersaint Kanva).
There is a river called Malini River which flows beside the Aashram of Maharishi Kanva.

The environment of Kanvashram is very beautiful. It have forests, many varities of trees for the needs of the prople living here. To buy industrial products, it also consist a wide range area of city 'Kotdwara'.

The Aashram of Mahrarishi Kanva is surrounded by thick forest and some trees of banana and bates. To know more about the Aashrams of Kanvashram. you can click here Aashram.
